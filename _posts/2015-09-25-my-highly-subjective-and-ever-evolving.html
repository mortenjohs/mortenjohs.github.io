Even more important to arrive prepared! So, I made a list. Maybe it can be of interest for others. Who knows. (A BGG Geek List with the same info: https://www.boardgamegeek.com/geeklist/197261)

Main Releases

504

https://boardgamegeek.com/boardgame/175878/504
This year's Friedeman Friese seems fascinating. "504 is a game that creates 504 different games out of one box.", they say, but will any of them be worth our time (and shelf space (and money))?

7 Wonders: Duel

https://boardgamegeek.com/boardgame/173346/7-wonders-duel
Antoine Bauza teams up with Bruno Cathala to revisit "7 Wonders" as a two player game with intriguing drafting mechanics inspired by... pyramid solitaire.

Barony

https://boardgamegeek.com/boardgame/167513/barony
Splendor is one of my favourite games from last year. Marc André is back with a seemingly (slightly) heavier game.

Mysterium

https://boardgamegeek.com/boardgame/181304/mysterium
Coop Ghost Murder Mystery Dixit! (Technically it was at Essen last year, but only in Polish.)

Ships

https://www.boardgamegeek.com/boardgame/153737/ships
This year's Martin Wallace (A Study In Emerald, A Few Acres of Snow, Mythotopia...)

Bretagne

https://www.boardgamegeek.com/boardgame/179350/bretagne
Probably too heavy to get much play time these days, but it sounds intriguing.
http://opinionatedgamers.com/2015/09/21/liga-first-impressions-of-bretagne/

Inhabit the Earth

https://boardgamegeek.com/boardgame/181797/inhabit-earth

The Gallerist

https://boardgamegeek.com/boardgame/125153/gallerist

Steam Works

https://www.boardgamegeek.com/boardgame/143176/steam-works
Steampunk worker placement game.

Favor of the Pharaoh

https://boardgamegeek.com/boardgame/171011/favor-pharaoh
A new dice game from Tom Lehman. (Roll for the Galaxy, etc.)

Above and Below

https://www.boardgamegeek.com/boardgame/172818/above-and-below
The new Ryan Laukat sounds intriguing. City building meets story telling... (Not entirely sure this will be presented at Essen, but LudoVox seems to think so... )

Dice City

https://www.boardgamegeek.com/boardgame/179572/dice-city
"Dice crafting"?

Flip City

https://www.boardgamegeek.com/boardgame/168679/flip-city
Deckbuilding meets Minivilles? Push your luck with a card flipping gimmick... Why not?

Small City

https://www.boardgamegeek.com/boardgame/130486/small-city
Another mayor-sim?

My Village

http://www.boardgamegeek.com/boardgame/172381/
Dice-version of Village.

Pandemic Legacy

https://www.boardgamegeek.com/boardgame/161936/pandemic-legacy
Pandemic. Legacy.

Scythe

http://www.boardgamegeek.com/boardgame/169786/
The new game from Stonemaier Games. (Viticulture w/Tuscany, Euphoria, Between two Cities...)

Automobiles

https://www.boardgamegeek.com/boardgame/180680/automobiles
Third game in the series that started with the excellent Trains. A bag-builder. My collection could do with a bag builder...

Cthulhu Realms

http://www.boardgamegeek.com/boardgame/179303/
Star Realms meets Lovecraft? This must be tried.

Tides of Time

https://www.boardgamegeek.com/boardgame/176229/tides-time
Another two player drafting game... in a small box. From Portal Games.

Codenames

https://boardgamegeek.com/boardgame/178900/codenames
Another take on the Dixit association party game sub genre? From a fascinating designer. Why not?

Nippon

https://boardgamegeek.com/boardgame/154809/nippon

Dino Twist

https://boardgamegeek.com/boardgame/179723/dino-twist
To call it "6 nimmt" with donosaur theme would be unfair, but I had a quick introduction to this game at the Octogônes fair in Lyon this weekend and I am looking forward to try it for real.

Already Kickstarted

I have kickstarted a couple of games that might be demoed this year.

Epic

https://www.boardgamegeek.com/boardgame/175621/epic
From the makers of Star Realms.

Between Two Cities

https://www.boardgamegeek.com/boardgame/168435/between-two-cities
Stonemaier Games' first game from external designers. A fascinating take on drafting. Casual 20 minutes game for 1-7 players.

Black Hat

https://www.boardgamegeek.com/boardgame/148282/black-hat
Played this at Essen last year. Excellent take on trick taking.
